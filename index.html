Guiding Principles

Our architectural foundation ensures every AI solution we build is responsible, secure, and scalable.

  • ●
    Human in the Loop: AI will augment, not replace, the critical thinking and decision-making of our staff and academics.
  • ●
    Ethical & Transparent: Built upon a strict ethical framework to ensure fairness, accountability, and transparency.
  • ●
    Secure & Compliant: Designed with robust security and data privacy controls by default.
  • ●
    Integrated & Scalable: Pilots are building blocks for a future-proof, university-wide AI ecosystem, avoiding technology silos.

Three Pillars of Transformation

Our AI strategy is focused on delivering measurable impact across the three core missions of the university.

1

Enhance Teaching & Learning

Creating personalized, engaging, and accessible educational experiences for every student.

  • ▶ Digital Twin Campus: Virtual labs and architectural simulations.
  • ▶ Personalized Feedback: AI-powered tools for instant formative assessment.
  • ▶ AI-Tutor Chatbots: 24/7 support for student queries.
2

Optimize Admin Processes

Automating repetitive tasks to improve efficiency, reduce errors, and free up staff for high-value work.

  • ▶ Intelligent Operations: Predictive maintenance and optimized timetabling.
  • ▶ Automated Admissions: Faster processing of applications and transcripts.
  • ▶ Student Success Analytics: Proactive, data-driven support to improve retention.
3

Empower Research & Innovation

Providing powerful tools and platforms to accelerate discovery and elevate BCU's research profile.

  • ▶ AI for Grant Writing: Assistance in finding and drafting funding proposals.
  • ▶ "Grand Challenge" Initiatives: Fostering cross-faculty collaboration on major projects.
  • ▶ Centralized AI Platform: Reducing costs and maintenance for research projects.

Our 24-Month Roadmap

A phased approach to ensure successful delivery, learning, and scaling of our AI capabilities from pilot to production.

Q1

Foundation & Mobilisation

Establish the AI COE. Finalize ethical framework and architectural principles. Initiate procurement for pilot technologies.

Q2

Pilot Launch & Development

Launch "AI Chat Assistants" and "Co-pilot" pilots. Begin development of initial Robotic Process Automation use cases.

Q3

Implementation & Learning

Deploy immersive experience VR kits. Gather initial data and user feedback from all active pilots. First round of COE reviews.

Q4

Evaluation & Year 1 Review

Evaluate pilot ROI and performance against KPIs. Develop business cases for scaling successful initiatives in Year 2.

Q5

Scaling & Expansion

Begin wider rollout of successful pilots (e.g., Co-pilot to more faculties, automate new processes based on Year 1 learnings).

Q6

Integration & Enhancement

Integrate mature AI tools with core university systems (e.g., VLE, student records). Enhance chatbot capabilities with new data sources.

Q7

Innovation & Research Focus

Launch a cross-faculty "Grand Challenge" AI research initiative. Develop and pilot new "Digital Twin" use cases.

Q8

Operationalisation & Future Planning

Embed scaled services into standard university operations. Develop the long-term (3-5 year) AI strategy based on program learnings.
